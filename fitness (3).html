Why Fitness Matters to Me

Fitness has been a cornerstone of my life since high school. Whether it's hitting the gym before class or going on long trail runs on weekends, staying active keeps me focused and energized for everything else I do.

I believe a healthy body fuels a sharp mind. Consistent exercise has helped me manage stress, maintain discipline, and build the kind of confidence that carries over into school and work.

"Take care of your body. It's the only place you have to live." — Jim Rohn

My Weekly Routine

Consistency is everything. Here's how I structure my week to stay on track:

  1. Monday & Thursday — Strength Training
    • Chest, shoulders, and triceps — focus on progressive overload.
    • 60-minute sessions with compound lifts first.
    • Finish with a 10-minute core circuit.
  2. Tuesday & Friday — Back & Cardio
    • Back, biceps, and rear delts.
    • 20-minute cardio finisher — stairmaster or rowing machine.
    • Stretch for 10 minutes at the end.
  3. Wednesday & Saturday — Outdoors
    • Long run or hike depending on the weather.
    • Aim for 8–12 miles total on weekends.
    • Great trails in the Wasatch Range nearby.
  4. Sunday — Active Recovery
    • Light yoga or mobility work.
    • Meal prep for the week ahead.

Top Fitness Tips

Stay consistent. Showing up on days you don't feel like it is where real progress is made. Three average workouts beat one perfect workout every time.

Prioritize sleep. Recovery happens at night. Aim for 7–9 hours — it affects your performance more than almost anything else.

Track your progress. Keep a simple log of your lifts or runs. Seeing improvement over time is one of the best motivators there is.
